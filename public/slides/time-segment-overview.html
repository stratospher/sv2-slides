Attack 2: Time-Segment Reconnect

Vulnerability: Forced Reconnection Hijack
1
Normal Monitoring

MITM forwards traffic seamlessly. Miner is submitting shares to legit pool.

<-- mining.submit("legit_job")
--> result: true
2
Reconnect Injection ATTACK

MITM sends client.reconnect pointing to Malicious Pool.

--> client.reconnect("evil.pool", 3333, 0)
3
Malicious Mining

Miner disconnects from Legit Pool, connects to Evil Pool, and mines for attacker.

<-- mining.authorize("attacker_user", ...)
4
Covering Tracks

After time T, Evil Pool sends reconnect back to Legit Pool. Cycle repeats.

The Duty Cycle Attack

By exploiting the unauthenticated client.reconnect command, attackers force miners to work for them in short bursts, making it look like connection instability rather than theft.

Operational Symptoms

  • Frequent TCP connection resets observed on firewall.

  • Legit pool records hashrate dips (e.g., 20% lower average).

  • Miner logs show "Pool requested reconnect" - looks like normal load balancing.
