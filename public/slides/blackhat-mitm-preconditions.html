How Work Hijacking Is Possible

Three conditions that make SV1 vulnerable to on-path attacks

Attack topology — dual upstream sessions

On-Path Positioning

The attacker must sit between the miner and the pool — ISP, LAN, rogue Wi-Fi, or BGP hijacking — to intercept the TCP connection on port 3333.

Plaintext Visibility

Because Stratum V1 sends JSON in cleartext, the attacker can parse every message type in real time — subscribe, authorize, notify, submit.

Dual Upstream Sessions

The attacker maintains two active connections simultaneously:

Legit Pool — proxies normal traffic to keep the miner unsuspecting.
Evil Pool — receives injected jobs; stolen work credited to the attacker.

No detection possible in SV1 — the miner sees normal job flow and share acknowledgements throughout the entire attack.
