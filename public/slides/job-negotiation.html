Job Negotiation: Miner-Driven Templates

1

Miner Proposes Template

Unlike V1 where the pool decides everything, in V2 the miner selects transactions and constructs the block template (version, coinbase, merkle root).

2

Cryptographic Commitment

The miner calculates a commitment (hash) of the template and signs it. This is sent to the pool via the Job Negotiation subprotocol.

3

Pool Validation & Auth

The pool validates the proposal against its policy (e.g., fee requirements, whitelists). If valid, it returns a cryptographically signed authorization token.

Security Outcome

Job injection attacks are impossible because an attacker cannot forge the miner's signature on the proposal nor the pool's signature on the authorization. Any modification results in a signature mismatch.
