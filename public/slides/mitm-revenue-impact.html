Proof of Work Theft

The attacker intercepts the miner's solved work before it reaches the pool — claiming the block reward themselves. The miner's hardware runs at full power, but someone else earns the payout.

Below the Noise Floor

Stealing only 1–2% keeps losses within normal hashrate variance. The miner can't distinguish an active attack from routine underperformance — making detection practically impossible.

Existential for Thin-Margin Operations

With net margins of 5–15%, losing 2% of hashrate means losing ~20% of profit. For large farms running hundreds of petahash, this silently erodes the entire business case.

SV1 has no authentication and no encryption — any on-path attacker can read, intercept, and redirect mining sessions in real time.
