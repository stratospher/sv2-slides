Key Authenticity: Who Are You Talking To?

1

The Threat: Key Swapping (MITM)

Encryption alone guarantees confidentiality, not identity. An attacker can intercept the handshake, presenting their own key to the miner and the pool.

Result: You have a secure channel with the Attacker.

2

Approach A: Pre-Shared Keys (PSK)

Both sides agree on a secret password beforehand. Handshake fails if secret doesn't match.

Issue: Doesn't scale. Distributing secrets to 1M+ anonymous miners is impossible.

3

Approach B: Identity Keys (Stratum V2)

Pools publish a long-term Static Public Key (Identity). Miners "pin" or verify this key during the Noise handshake.

Result: Scalable authenticity without distributing secrets.
