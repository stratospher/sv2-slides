Key Reuse Risks

If the same code or seed is reused across connections, patterns emerge. Attackers can perform cryptanalysis to recover the plaintext or the key itself over time.

The Distribution Paradox

How do you share the seed? If sent over the same channel, eavesdroppers capture it immediately, rendering encryption useless.

The Requirement

We need a method to generate a fresh, unique key for every connection that both parties can derive independently, without ever transmitting the key itself.

Without a secure key exchange mechanism, encryption is just obfuscation.
